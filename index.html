What if Facebook had existed during the Russian Revolution?

Project 1917 is a documentary produced in a new format: a social network for the most prominent people of Russia’s 1917 revolution. The daily feed weaves together a patchwork of quotes from diaries, memoirs and other documents. The project broke new ground by publishing texts that have never been published before.

Posts cover the most gripping twists and turns of the revolution and feature archival photos and video. Project 1917 shows you history in motion through the eyes of those who lived through it.

We spread and informal information about the life of Russian society during the most important moment in our history, the moment that changed everything.

This simple-yet-compelling format could be easily applied to other historical epochs and countries.

1968. DIGITAL

A unique series about an exceptional year

About Website

A unique series about an exceptional year

About Website
Interactive videos, website and a mobile app in English, French and Russian.

“Future History Lab” and Bazelevs, an international film company, are producing documentary series in a never-before-seen format:

Series is produced in a vertical format suitable for mobile screens;

We introduce a new video technology called “screen-life”, where all the objects inside the episode are interactive and clickable;

Join us on a virtual trip to the year 1968, where the internet, smartphones, social media and apps rule. Each episode takes you through the story of a particular protagonist as if she had a mobile phone at the time of events.

We present the global outlook. We searched for parallels and coincidences in the events that broke out all over the world in the Americas, Europe, USSR, Vietnam, Latin America, Japan, and China.

Mikhail Zygar
Founder & Editor-in-Chief
Journalist and writer

Founding editor-in-chief of Dozhd (2010-2015), Russia’s only independent TV station. Author of international bestsellers All the Kremlin’s Men: Inside the Court of Vladimir Putin and The Empire Must Die: Russia’s Revolutionary Collapse. Known internationally for media and political expertise, Zygar received the International Press Freedom Award from the Committee to Protect Journalists in 2014.

Zygar is a founder of the creative studio “Future History Lab” and editor-in-chief of “Project 1917.”

THE EMPIRE MUST DIE

The Empire Must Die portrays the vivid drama of Russia's brief and exotic experiment with civil society before it was swept away by the despotism of the Communist Revolution. The window between two equally stifling autocracies — the imperial family and the communists — was open only briefly during the last couple of years of the 19th century until the end of WWI, by which time the revolution was in full fury.

A vivid, character-driven reconstruction of the period leading up to the overthrow of the Romanovs and the birth of modern Russia. The author commands a powerful depth of historical knowledge and a novelist’s knack for sorting through the details to determine what is important and what’s ancillary. His book is long on meaningful storytelling in the service of finding out what went wrong in Russia’s brief moment of liberalism, an era that snapped shut a century ago. Kirkus Review
